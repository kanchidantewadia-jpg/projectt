Our Story

How ReUn began and our journey to becoming a trusted platform for finding missing persons

Born from a Personal Experience

ReUn was founded in 2018 after our founder, Sarah Johnson, experienced the anguish of having a family member go missing. Her elderly father, who had early-stage dementia, wandered away from home and was missing for three terrifying days.

During those difficult days, Sarah realized how fragmented and inefficient the process of finding missing persons could be. While there were many well-intentioned efforts, there was no centralized platform that effectively coordinated community resources, technology, and official channels.

This personal crisis became the catalyst for creating ReUn - a comprehensive platform that leverages community power and technology to help families in their most vulnerable moments.

Our Growth and Impact

What started as a small local initiative has grown into a nationwide network with over 5,000 volunteers across 200+ cities. In just five years, we've helped reunite more than 1,250 missing persons with their families.

Our approach combines the latest technology with the power of human compassion. We've developed proprietary algorithms that help predict possible locations based on behavioral patterns, while our community network ensures rapid response in critical situations.

We continuously refine our processes based on data analysis and feedback from both families and law enforcement agencies to improve our success rates and response times.

Our Mission & Vision

The driving force behind everything we do at ReUn

Our Mission

To leverage technology and community power to quickly and efficiently reunite missing persons with their loved ones, providing hope and support during times of crisis.

Our Vision

A world where no family has to endure the anguish of a missing loved one alone, where communities are empowered and equipped to respond effectively to these emergencies.

Our Promise

To treat every case with the urgency, compassion, and dedication we would want for our own family members, using every resource available to bring people home safely.
